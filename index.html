Welcome to Lusaka South University College (LSUC)

At Lusaka South University College, we are dedicated to bridging the gap between academic theory and industry practice. We offer a comprehensive suite of academic and professional programmes ranging from Certificate and Diploma to Degree levels, ensuring that every learner finds a pathway to success.

Legal Recognition & Accreditation

Your future is safe with us. Lusaka South University College is a fully recognized and accredited training institution in Zambia. We operate under the legal authority of the Higher Education Authority (HEA) and the Technical Education, Vocational and Entrepreneurship Training Authority (TEVETA). These regulatory bodies ensure that our curriculum, faculty, and facilities conform to both stringent local and international educational standards.

Our History

Established in 2023, Lusaka South University College has grown from a small technical institution to a comprehensive college offering diverse programs. We have graduated over 100 students who are making significant contributions to Zambia's development.

Our Mission

To provide quality, accessible, and relevant education that equips students with practical skills, critical thinking abilities, and ethical values needed for personal growth and national development.

Our Vision

To be the leading institution of higher learning in Southern Africa, recognized for excellence in education, research, and community service that transforms lives and contributes to sustainable development.

Campus Structure

Our modern campus features state-of-the-art classrooms, well-equipped laboratories, a comprehensive library, sports facilities, and comfortable student accommodation, all designed to create an optimal learning environment.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about LSUC

Home > FAQs

How do I apply for admission?

Applications can be submitted online through our website or in person at the admissions office. Required documents include academic certificates, national ID, and passport photos. Application deadlines vary by program.

What are the admission requirements?

Requirements vary by program. Generally, diploma programs require Grade 12 certificate, while certificate programs may accept Grade 10 or 11. Specific grade requirements are listed for each program.

Are scholarships available?

Yes, we offer merit-based scholarships, need-based financial aid, and partnerships with various organizations providing sponsorship opportunities for eligible students.

Is accommodation available?

We have on-campus hostels for both male and female students. Private accommodation options are also available nearby. Hostel applications should be submitted early due to limited spaces.

What is the academic calendar?

The academic year runs from February to November with two semesters. There are mid-semester breaks and a longer holiday in December and January.

How can I check my results?

Results are available through the student portal. You'll receive login credentials upon enrollment. Results are also posted on notice boards in the academic buildings.

What career services do you offer?

Our career services include job placement assistance, career counseling, resume writing workshops, interview preparation, and maintaining connections with industry partners.

How do I pay school fees?

Fees can be paid at the finance office using cash, bank deposits, mobile money, or bank transfers. Payment plans are available for students who need to pay in installments.

The Essence of LSUC’s Former Presidents Scholarship

Scholarship Awards are more than just fee discounts — here’s the real purpose behind them

Honor Zambia’s Legacy Through Education

Each scholarship ties LSUC’s mission to the legacy of Zambia’s former Presidents. From KK’s “One Zambia, One Nation” to later presidents’ focus on economic growth, we say: “Education builds the Zambia they envisioned.” It connects students to national history and pride.

Make Quality Education Accessible

With discounts from 20% to 70%, we remove financial barriers. A 70% KK Award can cut a K10,000 tuition to just K3,000 — the difference between a youth staying home vs. becoming a skilled graduate. It’s practical empowerment, not just charity.

Reward Merit + Need + National Service

The tiered percentage reflects contribution: the 70% KK Award is the highest honor for the founding father’s vision, while the 20% ECL Award recognizes recent efforts to expand universities. Students choose an award that resonates with them, while LSUC supports 1,200 students across both intakes.

Build LSUC’s Brand as “Zambia’s Premium Institution”

Naming awards after presidents positions LSUC as a national institution, not just another college. It signals stability, patriotism, and alignment with Zambia’s development agenda. Parents trust it. Students feel proud.
